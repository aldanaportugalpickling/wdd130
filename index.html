Iquitos-Peru

Iquitos is the capital of the Loreto department, in the northwest of Peru, in the heart of the Amazon rainforest. It is the largest city in the world that cannot be accessed by road, only via river or air. Founded during the rubber boom, its architectural heritage includes iconic buildings such as the Iron House. At the heart of Iquitos lies the Plaza de Armas, a central square surrounded by beautiful colonial buildings and vibrant daily activities. It is an important cultural, economic, and tourist center, famous for its exotic cuisine and vibrant nightlife. Iquitos is the gateway to the Pacaya Samiria National Reserve, a protected area of tremendous biodiversity. Visitors can explore its fascinating fauna and flora, as well as enjoy its lively markets and local festivities.
